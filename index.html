🚂 Rail Frontier — Railroad Tycoon

Found a railroad company, connect towns and industries, run freight and passengers, and out-grow your rivals across the decades.

  1. Find profitable routes — inspect towns and industries (Info tab) to see what each produces and demands. Cargo flows through chains: coal + iron → steel → goods; logs → lumber; grain or livestock → food; ports import goods and export raw materials.
  2. Build stations (3) — pick a tier (Depot, Station, Terminal); bigger tiers reach further and hold more cargo. 7 upgrades an existing one.
  3. Lay track (2) connecting them. Trains run diagonals automatically; track over water builds a bridge.
  4. Plan before you commit — the Buy Train (4) and Route (5) tools preview the route's distance, the cargo that will flow, and the expected monthly profit as you click stops, and warn you about dead stops, idle mills or a mis-sized engine. Each locomotive shows its role (Express, Heavy freight, Workhorse…) so you pick the right tool for coal vs passengers.
  5. Read the map — inspect a town, industry or station to see recommended cargo opportunities, idle-mill warnings, and (for an industry) faint arrows to its supply-chain partners. Well-served towns climb tiers: village → town → city → metropolis.
  6. Manage the company — the Fleet tab shows profit per train and per route; the Cargo tab flags unserved demand and oversupplied cargo. Issue bonds in Finance, ride the boom/bust economy, and watch the Rivals tab — connect a contested town before they take its traffic.

Pan with right-drag / WASD. Zoom with the wheel. Click the minimap to jump. F follows a train. Space pauses. The game autosaves every two minutes.
